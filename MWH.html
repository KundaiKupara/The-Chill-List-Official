Manly Wormhole: A Secret Tunnel Between Beaches

Carved into the sandstone cliffs of Queenscliff Headland in 1908 by local fishermen, the Manly Wormhole—also known as the Queenscliff Tunnel—is a narrow, 40-metre passage that connects Queenscliff Beach to Freshwater Beach. Originally built to avoid the treacherous climb over rocks, the tunnel has become a local legend and a favourite among adventurous walkers and photographers.

What Makes it Special

- Historic Origins: Dug by hand over a century ago, the tunnel reflects the ingenuity of early coastal communities.
- Scenic Adventure: The walk to the wormhole involves scrambling over rocks along the headland, offering dramatic ocean views and a sense of discovery.
- Instagram-Worthy: The curved tunnel frames the sea beautifully, making it a popular spot for photos—especially at sunrise or sunset.
- No Signposts: True to its "secret" status, there are no official signs or paths leading to the wormhole. It's known mostly by locals and intrepid explorers.

How to Find It

Start from Queenscliff Rockpool near the Queenscliff Surf Life Saving Club. From there, follow the rocky shoreline around the headland.
Look for a bright pink heart painted near the entrance—a subtle marker that you're close. Be sure to attempt the walk during low tide, as waves can be unpredictable and the rocks slippery.
